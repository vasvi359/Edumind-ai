smart_toy

Certainly. The transition from the Early Renaissance to the High Renaissance marks a profound shift in how light and shadow were perceived as structural elements of a painting.

book_2 Key Terminology

Chiaroscuro

Derived from the Italian chiaro (light) and oscuro (dark). It is the use of strong contrasts between light and dark to give the illusion of three-dimensional volume on a two-dimensional surface.

While Early Renaissance painters like Giotto used light to define shape, it often felt localized. Leonardo took this further with his own development: Sfumato.

The subtle blending of Mona Lisa's gaze.
Dramatic contrast in late 15th-century works.

In Leonardo’s hands, chiaroscuro wasn't just about form; it was about atmosphere. The shadows don't just stop; they bleed into the darkness, creating a sense of mystery and depth that his predecessors lacked.
